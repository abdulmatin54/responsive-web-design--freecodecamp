Marie Curie

The woman who pioneered radioactivity

Here's a timeline of Marie Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1891 - Moves to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne
  • 1895 - Marries French physicist Pierre Curie
  • 1898 - Discovers the radioactive elements polonium and radium
  • 1903 - Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel
  • 1906 - Pierre Curie dies in a street accident; Marie takes over his teaching post at the Sorbonne, becoming the first female professor there
  • 1911 - Wins a second Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for isolating pure radium
  • 1914 - Opens the Radium Institute in Paris, a major center for nuclear physics and chemistry
